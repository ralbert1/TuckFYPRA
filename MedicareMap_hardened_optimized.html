Methodology & Reliability

Data Quality

Source

The data rendered on this map comes from the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Medicare Provider Utilization and Payment Data at the county level. This version reflects the 2023 source data and covers four high-volume surgical procedures reported at the provider ZIP-code level, aggregated here to county level for choropleth visualization.

  • HCPCS 22630 — Fusion of lower spine bone and partial removal of spine bone or disc through back, 1 disc.
  • HCPCS 27130 — Replacement of thigh bone and hip joint with prosthesis (total hip replacement).
  • HCPCS 29827 — Repair of shoulder rotator cuff using an endoscope.
  • HCPCS 58571 — Removal of uterus, tubes, and/or ovaries through abdomen using an endoscope, 250 g or less.

County Aggregation

The raw dataset contains multiple rows per county and procedure — one per provider-ZIP combination. These are aggregated to the county + procedure level by summing total_procedures and summing total_beneficiaries across all rows sharing the same county and procedure code.

Important: total_beneficiaries is procedure-specific — it counts beneficiaries who received that specific HCPCS code, not total Medicare beneficiaries in the county. It is shown in tooltips for reference only and is not used as a population denominator.

The county Medicare population denominator is Medicare_coverage_alone_or_in_combination — the total Medicare-covered population of that county. This value is a county-level constant and is not summed across rows; only one value per county is retained. Counties with a missing or zero denominator are excluded from scoring and rendered as no-data.

Volume Index Score

Goal: measure how strong a county is for one selected procedure by rewarding both scale and utilization relative to its Medicare population.

Step 1 — County utilization rate

county_utilization_rate = county_total_procedures / county_medicare_population

This adjusts raw procedure volume for the size of the county's Medicare-covered population.

Step 2 — National utilization rate for the same procedure

national_utilization_rate = sum(county_total_procedures across valid counties) / sum(county_medicare_population across valid counties)

Only counties with a valid denominator are included, and each county's Medicare population is counted once.

Step 3 — Relative performance index

index_score = county_utilization_rate / national_utilization_rate

  • 1.0 means the county is exactly at the national average for that procedure.
  • > 1.0 means the county is above the national average.
  • < 1.0 means the county is below the national average.

Step 4 — Normalize both size and utilization

Raw procedure volume and index score are each normalized separately to a 0–1 scale using the 5th–95th percentile range, which reduces the influence of extreme outliers.

Step 5 — Final Volume Index Score

Volume Index Score = 100 × √( normalized_volume × normalized_index_score )

Interpretation: a county scores well only when it has both meaningful raw volume and above-average utilization. A large county with weak utilization, or a small county with strong utilization but very little volume, will both score lower than a county that balances both.

Oath Opportunities (Cross-Procedure Breadth)

Goal: identify counties that are strong across multiple procedures, not just one isolated spike.

Step 1 — Start with each procedure's Volume Index Score

For every county and every one of the four tracked procedures, the model first computes the Volume Index Score described above.

Step 2 — Build a breadth score across procedures

breadth_raw = ( ∏ max(VolumeIndexScore_p, 0.001) )^(1 / 4)

The model uses the geometric mean across all four procedures. If a county has no activity for a procedure, that procedure contributes 0.001 instead of zero, which sharply penalizes narrow specialization.

Step 3 — Add total multi-procedure scale

total_multi_procedure_volume = sum(county_total_procedures across all four procedures)

This prevents a county from scoring highly on breadth alone if the underlying total volume is too small to matter.

Step 4 — Normalize breadth and total volume separately

Both the raw breadth score and total multi-procedure volume are normalized to a 0–1 scale using the 5th–95th percentile range.

Step 5 — Final Oath Opportunities Score

Oath Score = 100 × √( normalized_breadth_raw × normalized_total_multi_procedure_volume )

Interpretation: a county scores well only when it has both broad cross-procedure strength and meaningful total scale. Strong performance in just one procedure is not enough.

FIPS Mapping

The CSV contains county names (e.g., “Jefferson County, Alabama”) rather than FIPS codes. County FIPS codes are resolved at runtime by matching the county name against the TopoJSON geometry file's county name properties, cross-referenced by state FIPS code. Matching logic strips county-type suffixes (County, Parish, Borough, Census Area, Municipality, City and Borough) before lookup. Counties that cannot be matched are excluded and rendered as no-data.

Special cases handled: Alaska boroughs/municipalities/census areas; Louisiana parishes; Virginia independent cities; Connecticut planning regions (may appear blank if boundary file uses legacy county FIPS).

Missing Data & Exclusions

  • Counties with no procedures for the selected HCPCS code appear in the neutral no-data color.
  • Counties where Medicare_coverage_alone_or_in_combination is zero or missing are excluded from scoring.
  • Volume and index score normalization uses the 5th–95th percentile range; extreme outliers are clipped to the range boundaries rather than excluded.

Using This Map

The choropleth colors reflect computed county-level scores; no statistical significance testing is applied. A county shown in a high-scoring color reflects both above-average utilization and meaningful procedure volume relative to its Medicare population. For operational or research use, consult the underlying CMS data directly.
